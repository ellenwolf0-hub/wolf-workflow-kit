## Starter Lessons (Pre-seeded)

**L-1: Lead calibration summaries with growth themes before development areas.**
Starting with strengths puts the person in a growth mindset before hearing development feedback. This pattern holds for performance conversations, 9-box discussions, and written calibration notes.

**L-2: When drafting a sensitive communication (PIP, comp, separation), write the first draft without tone hedging — then ask to "soften" or "harden" as a second pass.**
Trying to calibrate tone on the first pass buries the substance. Write clearly first, adjust tone second.

**L-3: Meeting decisions and follow-ups are almost always more actionable than the discussion itself.**
When reviewing a meeting note, skip to `## Decisions` and `## Action Items` first. Everything else is context.

**L-4: Zapier webhooks can fail silently if the Zap isn't published.**
If you're not seeing Slack posts or Coda updates after `/meet` or `/closeout`, check that the Zap is Published (not Draft) in Zapier.

**L-5: The most effective `/draft` prompts include the audience, the goal, and one constraint.**
Example: "Draft a message to my manager (Brady) explaining the Metro kickoff delay (audience: skip-level, goal: transparency, constraint: under 150 words)." The more context you give, the less back-and-forth you need.
